Accessibility statement

This site is being built and maintained with accessibility in mind. The goal is for as many people as possible to use it comfortably, including people using assistive technologies.

Accessibility is an ongoing process, not a one-time project. This statement describes the current approach, what I’m aiming for, and how to report a problem.

Commitment to accessibility

I design and develop jeffreyplewak.com with the same principles I use for client work: clear structure, semantic HTML, and an intent to support assistive technologies wherever possible. The site is intended to be usable by people who navigate with a keyboard, screen reader, or other assistive tools, as well as on smaller screens and different zoom levels.

For legal and regulatory context, I follow public guidance on web accessibility and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) for businesses open to the public, which emphasize effective communication and equal access, even where there is flexibility in how those requirements are met.

Accessibility standard and target level

The primary technical standard I use as a reference is the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA. This is the level most often referenced in current accessibility guidance and case law for businesses. I also track guidance related to WCAG 2.2 as it becomes more widely adopted.

I do not claim perfect or full conformance. Instead, the goal is to move the site toward WCAG 2.1 AA over time, while being transparent about limitations and responsive to feedback. This aligns with current best practices that treat accessibility as a continuous improvement process.

What this site is doing today

The site is relatively simple – a small set of static pages, contact forms, booking links, and payment paths. Within that scope, I aim to:

  • Use clear and consistent navigation across all pages.
  • Structure content with headings, lists, and landmarks so screen readers can understand the layout.
  • Provide descriptive link text instead of “click here” where possible.
  • Ensure text can be enlarged using browser zoom and remains readable on small screens.
  • Maintain reasonable color contrast between text and background, especially for body text and buttons.
  • Keep forms as simple as possible, with visible labels and clear instructions.

Third-party services and known limitations

This site embeds or links to a few third-party services, such as:

  • Calendly (for booking calls).
  • HubSpot forms (for contact and intake), loaded from js-na2.hsforms.net.
  • Stripe, PayPal, and other payment providers (via hosted checkout pages or links).
  • Analytics and error-logging tools used to understand which parts of the site are working.

I do not control the code for these external services. Each vendor is responsible for their own accessibility, though I try to choose tools that state an accessibility commitment and to configure them in accessible ways. If an issue appears inside an embedded widget, I may need to work with that vendor or adjust the implementation.

Current status and ongoing work

At this time, the site has been built using semantic HTML, responsive layouts, and a color palette chosen with contrast in mind. It has been spot-checked using keyboard navigation and basic screen-reader and zoom checks, but it has not undergone a formal third-party accessibility audit yet.

As the site evolves – new pages, more examples, and deeper case studies – I plan to:

  • Periodically review templates and components against updated WCAG guidance.
  • Address issues raised by visitors as they are reported.
  • Seek external review where appropriate, especially as the site grows.

Feedback and contact for accessibility issues

If you have trouble using any part of this website, find something hard to read or operate, or discover a specific accessibility problem, please let me know. Useful details include:

  • The page or feature you were using (a URL helps).
  • What you were trying to do.
  • Any assistive technology or browser you were using (for example, screen reader, browser, device).

You can reach me at:

  • Email: plewak.jeff@gmail.com
  • Contact form: jeffreyplewak.com/contact.html
  • Or, if easier, mention the issue when you book an intro call: jeffreyplewak.com/book.html

I will review your message and, where possible, respond with either a fix, a workaround, or a realistic timeline for addressing the issue.

Legal note

This accessibility statement is intended to describe the current approach and commitments for jeffreyplewak.com. It is not legal advice. If you need a formal legal interpretation of accessibility obligations for your own organization, you should consult qualified counsel who can review your specific situation and the latest regulations relevant to your jurisdiction and industry.
